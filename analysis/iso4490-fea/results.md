## Thermal: steady-state wall temperature & expansion

**Reading:** the FE mean wall temperature matches the lumped h-balance closed form (insulated ends + uniform boundary conditions ⇒ no axial gradient, FE recovers the lumped answer to machine precision). Thermal-
expansion-induced orifice drift is **2–4 µm**, which is **6–16% of the ISO 4490 ±25 µm tolerance** — small 
but non-trivial for the AlSi10Mg / Carney case.

## Structural: gravity load of the powder column

Sample mass per standard (50 g Hall, 150 g Carney). Powder treated as a static column above the orifice; membrane-shell approximation for the cone wall.

| Geometry | Material | Powder | m (g) | p_max (Pa) | σ_hoop_max (kPa) | yield SF | Δd_orifice (µm) | frac of ISO tol |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| hall | alsi10mg | alsi10mg_powder | 50 | 684.5 | 4.4193 | 52044 | 0.000020 | 0.000001 |
| hall | 316ss | 316ss_powder | 50 | 1356.7 | 5.3103 | 38604 | 0.000014 | 0.000001 |
| carney | alsi10mg | alsi10mg_powder | 150 | 1521.9 | 16.9301 | 13585 | 0.000178 | 0.000007 |
| carney | 316ss | 316ss_powder | 150 | 2075.2 | 12.9321 | 15852 | 0.000088 | 0.000004 |

**Reading:** powder-column gravity load produces **sub-micron orifice deflection** (max 0.18 µm in the Carney/AlSi10Mg case), well below the ISO 4490 ±25 µm tolerance — `frac of ISO tol` < 1e-5 in every configuration. Hoop stresses are 4–17 kPa, several orders of magnitude below yield (safety factor ≫ 10⁴). 
The cone is mechanically *over-designed* for this load class; gravity is not the governing structural concern.

## Headline

Across both physics, the funnel geometry stays well inside the ISO 4490 ±25 µm orifice tolerance under the modeled powder-handling conditions. Thermal expansion is the dominant contributor (6–16% of tolerance) and dominates over gravity load (< 0.001% of tolerance). Both findings are produced by 1D linear-
FE solvers verified against closed-form references.
